Hades solves the fundamental tension of roguelikes — the repetition that's the point is also the thing that eventually exhausts you — by making each run advance a story. Zagreus is trying to escape the underworld. He fails repeatedly. The failures accumulate into relationships, revelations, and eventually resolution.

The boon system is deep enough to support genuine strategizing without becoming a spreadsheet exercise. The combat is fast without being frantic. The voice acting is extraordinary.

I completed the full narrative in about 40 hours and still wanted more. Coming for Hades II next.
